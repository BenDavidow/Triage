Why is chicken consumption responsible for so much suffering? Because chickens are so much smaller than cows and pigs. To get the same amount of meat that can be obtained from a single cow (or four pigs), more than 200 chickens must be killed. Additionally, chickens (both raised for meat and eggs) tend to be treated more severely than other farm animals. As Nick Cooney and I argue here, we reduce more suffering and protect more animals from harm when we focus our outreach on chickens and eggs. And we may have a greater impact if we tell people to reduce consumption of chickens (and eggs) rather than all animal products.
